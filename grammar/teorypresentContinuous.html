Present Continuous

The present continuous is used when an action is not finished yet, something is happening right now.

Pronouns Verb to be Verb-ing
I am working
He is
She
It
You are
We
They

Spelling

a. When a verb ends in e, it's changed to ing.
  • Come → Coming
  • Write → Writing
  • Dance → Daning
b. When a verb ends in consonant and the word sound like monosyllable, the last letter is duplicated and added ing.
  • Run → Running
  • Sit → Sitting
  • Swim → Swimming
c. When a verb ends in ei, it's changed to y and added ing.
  • Lie → Lying

Examples:

  • She's having lunch.
  • It's raining.
  • The athletes are running.
  • You're playing a video game.
  • We're listening.
  • I'm studying for a test.
  • The children are doing their homework.
  • He's writing a love letter.
  • They're swimming in the pool.
  • It's snowing there.
